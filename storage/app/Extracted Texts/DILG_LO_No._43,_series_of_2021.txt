Dear Gov. Suarez:
This has reference to your letter dated 10 October 2020 addressed to Usec. Epimaco V. Densing III concerning a request for opinion previously forwarded to the DILG Central Office by DILG Regional Office IV-A.
Per your letter the request is for an enlightenment on the validity of circumstances surrounding several sanggunian members' manner of constituting the new majority and minority, as well as reorganization of the committees in the sangguniang panlalawigan.
At the outset, premised on the principle of presumption of validity of the actions of local legislative bodies, we may not be able to pronounce/decide on the validity of the circumstances as mentioned. However, for purposes of academic discussion may we discuss that the governing provision of law on this matter is Section 50 of R.A. No. 7160, otherwise known as the "Local Government Code of 1991", which empowers the Sanggunians to adopt their respective Internal Rules of Procedure (IRP). Thus, may it be respectfully known that the query concerning the validity of the actions adopted by the members of the Sangguniang Panlalawigan can be answered based on the concerned sanggunian's IRP.
We ought to add that as explained in DILG Opinion No. 76 s. 2007, the sources of parliamentary rules of procedure of the sanggunian as a legislative or deliberative body are arranged in the order of hierarchy, to wit: 1.) The 1987 Constitution; 2.) The Local Government Code; 3.) Judicial Decisions; 4.) Adopted Internal Rules of Procedure of the Sanggunian; 5.) Parliamentary Practice; 6.) Parliamentary Authors; and 7.)
